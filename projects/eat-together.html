Background

The project, titled Eat Together, was developed to address critical issues faced by the homeless community, particularly resource wastage, uneven distribution, and the overemphasis on certain resources. These inefficiencies often led to an imbalance in how support was provided, leaving some individuals underserved while others received excess.

The inspiration behind this initiative came from collaborative research with HomeStreetHome Taiwan, which highlighted these gaps in resource management and distribution. The project aimed to create a more tailored and efficient solution to ensure that resources reached those in need in a balanced manner.

By working closely with NGOs, the project sought to align technological solutions with the real-world needs of homeless individuals, making the process of receiving and distributing resources more streamlined and impactful.

Design

Swipe for Full Story:

The design of the Eat Together platform was centered on ease of use and accessibility for both NGO administrators and the homeless community. The following key design elements were implemented:

  • Calendar View: The core feature, developed using FullCalendar.js, displays resources by location, type, and time. Users can easily hover over events to see details such as resource type, quantity, and provider information.
  • Map Integration: The map section uses a carousel view to display street-level images of distribution points, making it easier for users to locate resources. This feature helps avoid logistical challenges and overcrowding at distribution locations.
  • Mobile-First Design: Special attention was given to mobile accessibility, as many users may rely on shared devices or public internet access. The design ensures the platform functions seamlessly across desktops and mobile phones.
  • NGO and Public Usability: The interface was designed to be intuitive for NGO administrators managing resources and public users seeking them, ensuring both groups can efficiently interact with the platform.

The design of the Eat Together platform was developed with a strong focus on usability, accessibility, and responsiveness, particularly to accommodate users from both NGO administrators and the general public. The platform is divided into distinct sections: the header, calendar, map, and footer, each serving a specific function.

  • Header Section: This section welcomes users with a dynamic flip animation showcasing the project’s title, slogan, and purpose. The design here is meant to make a strong first impression and convey the project’s core mission of efficient and equitable resource distribution.
  • Calendar Section: As the central feature of the platform, the calendar was designed using FullCalendar.js. It displays registered resources based on the time, date, and location of their distribution. Users can hover over events to see detailed information, including resource type, quantity, location, and provider.

    The sidebar lists events by resource category, allowing users to easily identify oversupplied or undersupplied resources. The calendar design incorporates tabs that allow users to switch between different distribution locations. By integrating these features, the calendar section ensures that both distributors and recipients can efficiently manage and access resources.
  • Map Section: The map displays distribution locations with both map and street views using a carousel. This is particularly useful for both distributors and the homeless community, as it helps them navigate to the correct locations. This section minimizes confusion and logistical issues like overcrowding, incorrect distribution points, and resource mismanagement. The top three gathering locations were chosen based on where the homeless community most frequently resides, ensuring that resource distribution is highly effective.
  • Footer Section: The footer provides basic contact details, social links, and donation opportunities for HomeStreetHome Taiwan, ensuring continued support and communication between NGOs and the public.

In addition, the platform was designed to adapt seamlessly to desktop and mobile versions, ensuring it remains accessible for users, particularly the homeless community, who may share mobile devices and rely on community internet access.

Implementation

The project was implemented using a full-stack approach, focusing on scalability and operational effectiveness for both NGO administrators and public users in real-world environments. The development process was independently led from start to finish, including pitching the concept to NGOs, designing the system’s architecture, coding, testing, and final deployment.

  • Front-End: Vue.js was selected for its flexibility, ease of integration, and reactive capabilities, allowing for a seamless and responsive user experience. This framework was critical in ensuring that users, from NGOs to the general public, could navigate the platform effortlessly across devices.
  • Back-End: Firebase was chosen for hosting and database management due to its smooth integration with Vue.js and its ability to scale alongside the platform’s growing user base. Firebase provided a reliable back-end solution without requiring complex server management.

Future

Phase 2 of the "Eat Together" project will introduce two key features:

  • Demand Registry: A new form will allow the homeless community to list their most urgent needs, which will be displayed in a sidebar for better resource distribution.
  • Location Pinning: The homeless can voluntarily pin their locations on a map, helping medical and social services provide targeted follow-up care.

These features were delayed due to the city government's involvement, which expanded the project’s scope. Ongoing research and feedback will shape their integration, further enhancing the platform’s community impact.

As the "Eat Together" project continues to evolve, we have identified two key features for future implementation, set to be introduced in Phase 2:

  • Demand Registry for the Homeless Community: Alongside the current resource registry form for distributors, a new form will allow the homeless community to communicate their specific needs directly. This "demand registry" will be visible in a sidebar, providing an intuitive snapshot of the most urgent resources required by the community. By clearly displaying the needs of the homeless, we aim to foster a more efficient and balanced distribution system that responds directly to these demands.
  • Location Pinning for Street Services: To improve street medical care and social services, Phase 2 will introduce a feature allowing the homeless community to pin their locations on the map. This will facilitate follow-up medical treatments, ongoing social support, and outreach programs. The feature will be voluntary, ensuring privacy while enabling social care providers to better target their efforts.

These features were initially postponed due to the expanded project scope following the city government’s involvement, which has broadened the platform’s potential impact. Discussions are ongoing to align the platform with the government’s objectives. Meanwhile, field research and user feedback will guide the integration of these features into the existing system. While Phase 1 established a strong foundation, Phase 2 will further enhance the platform’s community-focused and personalized impact.

Closing

Eat Together has garnered attention from local governmental bodies, including the Taichung City Government and the Social Affairs Bureau. These discussions have highlighted the platform’s potential not only to address immediate challenges but also to shape public policy and urban management by optimizing resource distribution processes.

As the platform moves into Phase 2, it remains flexible and scalable, poised to deepen its impact through enhanced communication between the homeless community and service providers. By building on the strong foundation established in Phase 1, Eat Together is well-positioned to drive meaningful change in both resource management and public welfare, ensuring continued support for the more vulnerable populations.
